The Tale of the Dragon and the Three Brothers

A chavacano folktale

From “Zamboangueno Texts with Grammatical Analysis; A Study of Philippine Creole Spanish. A Thesis Presented to the Faculty of the Graduate School of Cornell University for the Degree of Doctor of Philosophy”

by Michael Lawrence Forman, 1972.


Long ago, in a peaceful kingdom where happiness knew no quarrels, a twelve-headed dragon claimed a human life each year.


This time, the dragon took the king’s youngest and most beautiful daughter. The king made a vow: whoever could free her would gain her hand—and the throne itself.


Many tried. All failed. Until three brothers set sail: a marksman who never missed, a tailor who could sew faster than blood could spill, and a thief so skilled even a dragon’s eyes could not see him.


But when courage, skill, and fate collide… who truly deserves the princess?
